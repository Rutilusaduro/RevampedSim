# Skill: Character Craft — A Cast Worth Fattening

Characters are the product. The same gain arc lands completely differently
through different women, and that difference is what makes a cast game
replayable. This skill defines the axes and the voice discipline. All
characters are adults; see the pack README's hard rules.

## 1. The character sheet (minimum viable)

```
name, age (18+), role/archetype, body type, starting weight
appetite baseline, favorite indulgence
gain stance (see §3), psych axes starting values (see §4)
voice contract (see §6): 3 vocabulary notes, 1 rhythm note, 1 taboo topic
arc one-liner: where she starts → what she becomes → what turns her
wardrobe identity: 3 garments she's known for
```

## 2. Archetype × body type = silhouette identity

Pick archetypes for *behavioral* contrast (athlete, bookworm, influencer,
gamer, artist, sorority queen, chef, librarian, scientist…). Assign each a
**body type** that shapes WHERE gain shows: pear, apple, hourglass,
top-heavy, athletic-softening, evenly-rotund, bottom-heavy. Two rules:

- No two main characters share archetype AND body type.
- Body type changes the *prose*, not the *numbers* — same ladder, different
  geography ("her lower half sets the pace" vs "her belly arrives first").

## 3. Gain stance — how she relates to gaining (early game)

The axis that buys the most early-game variety:

| Stance | Reads as | Signature beats |
|---|---|---|
| **opposed** | actively resists, diets, denies | mirror arguments, secret weigh-ins, defiance-then-lapse |
| **reluctant** | conflicted, negotiating | "just this once", mixed signals, wanting to be convinced |
| **secret** | wants it, hides wanting it | private waistband checks, staged "accidents", blushing at notice |
| **neutral** | genuinely unfussed | notes changes and moves on; comedy potential |
| **eager** | openly enjoys it | asks for more, celebrates thresholds, recruits help |

Stances are STARTING positions; arcs move characters between them. The
reluctant→eager arc is the genre's bread; an opposed character who never
moves is a wall — give every stance a lever the player can find.

## 4. Psychology axes (the depth engine)

Track 3–5 axes as 0–3 tiers. Reference set:

- **fixation** — how much she thinks about food/eating.
- **obsession** — how much she thinks about her own changing body.
- **shame** — how much observation stings (a dial the arc turns DOWN or
  transforms into display).
- **dependence** — how much she needs the player's involvement.
- **corruption/ownership** (master axis if you want just one): denial →
  acclimating → owned. Every dialogue system keys on it.

Rules: tier changes fire scenes (never silent); axes interact (high shame +
high fixation = the secret eater; low shame + high obsession = the mirror
queen); dialogue pools key on tiers so the same question gets
tier-different answers.

## 5. NPC reaction ecology

A world that reacts multiplies every gain. Give each supporting character a
**reaction stance**: admiring, envious, oblivious, competitive, enabling,
flustered. Reactions scale with the subject's stage and shift with repeated
exposure (the roommate stops noticing; the rival starts). Group scenes
where two NPCs react differently to the same body are cheap, dense
characterization.

## 6. Voice contracts (dialogue discipline)

Per character, write and enforce:

- **Vocabulary:** 5–10 words/phrases she owns ("iconic", "statistically",
  "sweetheart"); 3 she would never say.
- **Rhythm:** short and clipped / rambling / precise / trailing-off.
- **Taboo:** the one topic she deflects (until the arc says otherwise) —
  deflections are characterization gold.
- **Body-distance register:** which gain-talk register she's currently
  earned (talks-around-it / names-it / advertises-it), keyed to her psych
  tiers, never skipped ahead.

Test: strip the name tags from a page of dialogue; a reader who knows the
cast should re-attribute 80%+.

## 7. The arc voice ladder (interior voice across the master axis)

For each main character, write a 6-beat voice guide across the master
psych axis BEFORE writing her interior/dialogue content. The beats:

1. **Baseline, first visible change** — her stance in action: excuses vs.
   unfussed vs. body contradicting her words.
2. **First crack** — pleasure where dread was expected; she files the
   feeling away; it does not stay filed.
3. **Mid-tier ambivalence** — stopped fighting, not yet celebrating; flat
   familiarity with the changes.
4. **The turn** — surrender with a social witness; the interior argument
   goes quiet; nothing left to negotiate.
5. **Settled ownership** — appetite open, body as project or status
   report; wants named plainly.
6. **Ceiling reality** — practical knowledge of her own scale; logistics
   as fact, not emergency.

Archetype colors each beat (a competitor keeps score, a chef catalogs
flavor, an analyst names the dynamic) but the axis position is primary.
Six lines per character; every interior/dialogue pool you write later
calibrates against them. This is the cheapest tool that keeps a cast from
converging into one voice at high tiers.

## 8. Persona lines vs generic lines (content economics)

You cannot hand-write every line for every girl at every state. The split
that scales: **persona lines** (unique voice, keyed to the character, used
sparingly at high weight) pooled with **trait-keyed generics** (keyed to
stance/psych/stage, using name substitution) that cover everyone. Aim for
persona lines at emotional peaks (rung crossings, firsts, endings) and
generics for connective tissue. Every character deserves ≥2 persona lines
per psych tier in every scene type that carries personality.

**Dialogue is sacred.** When reusing or migrating handwritten lines, keep
quoted speech verbatim — the voice lives in the quotes. Narration around
quotes may be genericized; the quotes themselves may not be paraphrased.

## 9. Arc design

An arc = stance trajectory × psych trajectory × weight trajectory, and the
three should NOT move in lockstep — the interesting characters are the
misaligned ones (body far ahead of acceptance; acceptance far ahead of
body; dependence growing while shame pretends otherwise). Plot each main
character's three lines across the game's timeline on one page. Where lines
cross, scenes live.
