# GRAVITY — Concept Document

Derived from the owner interview of 2026-07. Part I is the distilled
desire profile (keep it — it calibrates every future game, not just this
one). Part II is the game. Build order and open questions at the end.
House rules bind throughout: adults only, consensual, no health-decline
framing, charged-sensual heat ceiling.

---

## Part I — Owner desire profile (interview findings)

**Peak heat, ranked by evidence:**
1. The psychological flip — the moment resistance quietly dies.
2. Physical consequence made concrete: busting through things, getting
   stuck, becoming too fat for specific tasks. All three currents at
   once: the irreversible ratchet (losses stay lost), helpless heat in
   the stuck moment itself, and witnessed failure.
3. The threshold-give — the button, the seam, the chair.

**The calibration scene (owner's own words, condensed):** a woman too
fat for the door; friends pushing from the other side while she devours
ice cream by the bucket and visibly grows; the friends have been trying
to convince her she's too fat and are clearly failing; one friend has
gotten fat herself from her influence. Decoded, this scene carries four
loads: she indulges THROUGH the intervention (the flip already happened;
the world argues on anyway) · she grows DURING the event (spurt-in-scene,
the crisis worsening in real time) · opposition exists to lose · her
gravity spreads to the people near her.

**Structure:** one woman at a time, all the way down — then the next.
Anthology of deep single-subject arcs, not a managed cast. Player seat
varies per arc (partner, secret enabler, influenced friend, or
inhabiting her first-person). Finished women stay in the world.

**The loop that grips:** two-threshold probability windows — a weight
where an event becomes possible, a weight where it becomes certain,
rolled on every relevant action, fullness-weighted. Anticipation as UI.

**The gap Professor Sim leaves:** world reactivity (all four layers:
physical scarring, social graph, institutions, her own workarounds —
owner: "these all rock, huge focus") and compulsion-loop purity.

**Scale:** grounded core, ~180–500 lbs, where task-failure granularity
is maximal; overdrive beyond that optional, post-arc.

**Tone:** charged and sensual, house voice. Not comedy-first. Climax
events must vary heavily per woman — the door scene is one archetype,
never the template.

## Part II — The game

### Premise

One contemporary town. Every so often a woman in it falls into gravity —
an appetite that stops apologizing. You are someone in her orbit (who you
are changes per arc), and over one deep arc she outgrows her clothes, her
furniture, her routines, her town's arguments, and finally some signature
piece of the world that could not hold her. Then she stays. The town
rebuilds around her, her influence keeps radiating, and somewhere in the
crowd at her crown event, the next woman is watching with her mouth
slightly open. The game is the town losing the same argument, beautifully,
forever — and softening a little more each time it loses.

### The core loop

Day cycle. Observe her (portrait, windows panel) → spend the day's
actions (meals, outings, errands, conversations — with her or around
town) → every relevant action rolls the open probability windows →
consequences render, thresholds land, the world reacts → the windows
panel shows what just moved closer. One more day, one more meal, just to
watch the next window open: that is the whole hook, and it is enough.

### Signature mechanics

1. **Probability windows (the owner's mechanic, verbatim).** Every
   physical failure event — button, waistband, chair, booth, doorway,
   bathtub, car seat, stair rail, bed slat — carries two thresholds: the
   weight where it becomes POSSIBLE and the weight where it becomes
   CERTAIN. Chance ramps between them and rolls on every relevant
   action, modified by fullness, pace, and garment integrity. The UI
   shows open windows and approaching ones. Anticipation is the
   interface.
2. **The ratchet.** A failed object or lost capability stays failed:
   logged on her record, marked on the town map, referenced by NPCs
   weeks later. The save file is a scar history.
3. **Spurt-in-scene.** Major events carry live gain — she ends scenes
   measurably heavier than she entered them, and windows can open (and
   fire) mid-scene. The stuck-in-the-door crisis that worsens while
   they pull is the design target.
4. **Gravity (contagion).** Past a threshold, a woman radiates. Named
   NPCs in her orbit drift heavier; the drift is visible, gradual, and
   never stops. Finished women keep radiating forever. A town-softening
   index rises across the anthology — menus, chair stock, fashion,
   norms. And the next arc's subject EMERGES from a previous arc's
   orbit: the influenced friend of arc N is the candidate pool of arc
   N+1. The anthology chains itself.
5. **The argument (opposition system).** Friends stage interventions,
   gyms send reminders, mothers call. Opposition shapes scenes and
   exists to lose — but it loses on-screen, escalating from concern to
   bargaining to awed acceptance, and it loses a little faster each arc
   as the town softens. The narration never sides with it; the town
   argues out of love and is simply wrong.
6. **Seat assignment.** Each arc defines who you are: her partner, the
   secret enabler inside the concerned circle, the influenced friend
   being pulled in yourself, or her — first person. The seat is part of
   the woman's story design, chosen for maximum voltage with her
   particular arc.
7. **Crown events.** Every woman carries a signature climax class, hers
   alone: the wedding-dress fitting, the stage that gives way, the
   pool ladder, the airplane seat, the record buffet, the public scale
   ceremony, the door. Windows converge on it; the whole arc is
   secretly aimed at it; it is never the same event twice in a row.

### Arc anatomy

Stance start varies per woman (opposed, reluctant, secret, eager). Every
arc passes through its own flip — placed early for some women, late and
climactic for others. After the flip, the register shifts to the
calibration scene's key: serene, unstoppable, indulging through the
noise. Arc closes at her crown event plus an epilogue woven from her
ratchet log. Then she persists: a fixture, a landmark, a gravity source.
Optional per finished woman: **overdrive** — she keeps growing slowly in
the background of future arcs, the town forever re-rebuilding around
her.

### The compulsion stack (why you can't stop playing)

Visible windows opening (anticipation) + per-action rolls (variance) +
permanent ratchet (progress that can't un-happen) + gravity drift
(background numbers always creeping) + next-subject emergence (curiosity
about who's next) + town-softening index (meta-progression) + one
promised crown event per arc (narrative payoff). Seven interlocking
reasons to take one more day.

### Production notes

- Engine: the Text Engine Manifesto system as built; this concept is the
  `12-any-game` intake, pre-answered by the interview.
- Beat families: portrait · meals · interventions (new family) ·
  window-fire events (stuck/break/task-fail, with witnessed variants) ·
  gravity beats (the friend noticing herself) · town-ambient · ritual
  measurement · crown events · epilogues.
- Ladder: dense rungs across 180–500 (the grounded band needs the most
  granularity the project has ever authored — every 25–35 lbs should
  change what fails).
- Prose: house voice, charged-sensual, per `02` and the exemplar
  gallery; the door scene's register (defiant serenity mid-crisis) gets
  its own exemplar before content production.

### Vertical slice (build first)

One woman — suggest a "reluctant, late-flip" design so the slice proves
the argument system and the flip — one neighborhood of the town, eight
window event classes, one intervention chain, her crown event, gravity
drift on two named friends, seat: secret enabler. Slice is done when a
player who owns the fantasy reaches the crown event and immediately
wants to know which friend is next.

### Open questions (owner calls)

1. Town name and cast size for v1 (recommend: 3 chained arcs).
2. Art: text-only vs. portraits (concept works text-only; portraits
   amplify the ratchet if budgeted).
3. Session grain: strict day cycle vs. freeform scene picker
   (recommend day cycle for loop purity).
4. Whether overdrive is default-on or per-woman opt-in.

### Risks to watch

- **Gravity scope creep:** keep contagion as simple drift meters +
  keyed prose, never a second full simulation.
- **Intervention tone:** the argument must read as love losing to joy;
  one wrong register and it reads mean. Exemplar-gate this family
  first.
- **Door-scene monoculture:** crown-event variance is a hard rule;
  the lint pack should track climax-class distribution across arcs.
- **Grounded-band coverage:** 180–500 with per-25-lb consequence
  granularity is the densest authoring target yet; the window system
  only sings if every band has failures to offer.
